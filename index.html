Learn More About Us

Our Mission

We are dedicated to creating an inclusive community where students can explore robotics, develop technical skills, and collaborate on innovative projects. Our mission is to inspire the next generation of engineers, programmers, and innovators who will shape the future of technology.

What We Do

  • Weekly hands-on workshops and technical sessions
  • Collaborative robot building projects
  • Competition team training and support
  • Guest speakers from industry and academia
  • Networking events and career development
  • Access to tools, equipment, and resources

Who Can Join?

All students are welcome, regardless of experience level! Whether you've never touched a robot before or you're a seasoned competitor, we have something for everyone.

No experience required! We provide all the training and support you need to get started.

Our club meets every week during the academic year, and we offer various levels of involvement to fit your schedule.

Skills You'll Learn

🔧 Mechanical Design

CAD modeling, 3D printing, machining, and mechanical systems

💻 Programming

Python, C++, Arduino, ROS, and embedded systems

⚡ Electronics

Circuit design, sensors, motors, and control systems

Fellowship Program

About the Fellowship

Our Fellowship Program is designed for dedicated members who want to take their robotics expertise to the next level. Fellows receive specialized training, mentorship, and the opportunity to lead major club initiatives.

Program Benefits

  • One-on-one mentorship from industry professionals
  • Advanced technical workshops and training
  • Leadership opportunities within the club
  • Funding for personal robotics projects
  • Priority access to competitions and events
  • Networking with alumni and industry partners
  • Certificate of completion for your portfolio

Program Structure

Duration: One academic year (Fall & Spring)

Time Commitment: 8-10 hours per week

Cohort Size: 15-20 Fellows per year

Fellows work on a capstone project throughout the year, culminating in a showcase event where they present their work to the university community and industry partners.

Eligibility & Application

Who Can Apply:

  • Active club members for at least one semester
  • Demonstrated passion for robotics and technology
  • Strong collaboration and communication skills
  • All majors and class years welcome

Application Period: Applications open each Spring for the following academic year

Selection Process: Rolling applications, interview required

Workshops

Hands-On Learning

Our workshops are designed to teach practical skills in a fun, collaborative environment. No matter your skill level, you'll find workshops that challenge and inspire you.

Beginner Workshops

Introduction to Robotics

What You'll Learn:

  • Basic robot components and systems
  • Simple programming concepts
  • Building your first robot

Duration: 2 hours | Frequency: Monthly

Arduino Basics

What You'll Learn:

  • Microcontroller fundamentals
  • Reading sensors and controlling outputs
  • Writing and uploading code

Duration: 2 hours | Frequency: Monthly

CAD for Robotics

What You'll Learn:

  • 3D modeling basics
  • Designing robot parts
  • Preparing models for 3D printing

Duration: 3 hours | Frequency: Bi-monthly

Electronics 101

What You'll Learn:

  • Circuit fundamentals
  • Using breadboards and components
  • Safety and troubleshooting

Duration: 2 hours | Frequency: Monthly

Advanced Workshops

ROS (Robot Operating System)

What You'll Learn:

  • ROS architecture and concepts
  • Publishing and subscribing to topics
  • Building autonomous systems

Duration: 4 hours | Frequency: Quarterly

Computer Vision & AI

What You'll Learn:

  • Image processing with OpenCV
  • Object detection and tracking
  • Implementing ML models on robots

Duration: 4 hours | Frequency: Quarterly

Advanced Motor Control

What You'll Learn:

  • PID control theory
  • Servo and stepper motor control
  • Motion planning algorithms

Duration: 3 hours | Frequency: Bi-monthly

PCB Design

What You'll Learn:

  • Schematic capture
  • PCB layout and routing
  • Manufacturing considerations

Duration: 4 hours | Frequency: Quarterly

Workshop Schedule

Workshops are held weekly throughout the academic year. Check our events calendar or bulletin board for the latest schedule. All workshops are free for club members!

Competitions

Compete at the Highest Level

Our competition teams represent the best of what our club has to offer. Whether you're interested in building combat robots, autonomous vehicles, or solving complex challenges, we have a team for you.

Major Competitions

🏆 VEX Robotics Competition

About:

The VEX Robotics Competition is a global robotics competition where teams design, build, and program robots to compete in an engineering challenge.

Our Record:

  • 3x Regional Champions
  • National Semifinalists 2024
  • Excellence Award Winners

Season: September - April

Team Size: 8-12 members

Skills Needed: Mechanical design, programming, strategy

Commitment: 10-15 hours/week during competition season

🤖 FIRST Robotics Competition

About:

FIRST Robotics combines the excitement of sport with the rigors of science and technology. Teams build large-scale robots to compete in a high-energy game.

Our Achievements:

  • Regional Finalists 2025
  • Engineering Inspiration Award
  • Rookie All-Star Team

Season: January - April

Team Size: 15-25 members

Skills Needed: All disciplines - mechanical, electrical, programming, business

Commitment: 15-20 hours/week, intensive build season

🚗 Autonomous Vehicle Challenge

About:

Design and build an autonomous vehicle capable of navigating complex courses, avoiding obstacles, and completing specific tasks without human intervention.

Our Progress:

  • Qualified for Nationals 2025
  • Innovation Award Winners

Season: Year-round

Team Size: 6-10 members

Skills Needed: Computer vision, AI/ML, sensors, control systems

Commitment: 8-12 hours/week

⚔️ BattleBots / Combat Robotics

About:

Build armored robots designed to battle head-to-head in an arena. Focus on durability, strategy, and creative weaponry.

Our Highlights:

  • Local Tournament Champions
  • Fan Favorite Award 2024

Season: October - March

Team Size: 4-8 members

Skills Needed: Mechanical design, materials science, strategy

Commitment: 6-10 hours/week

How to Join a Competition Team

  • Attend an informational meeting at the start of the season
  • No tryouts required - all interested members welcome!
  • Teams welcome both beginners and experienced members
  • Mentorship provided for new team members
  • Flexible roles available based on your interests and schedule

Competition Funding: Travel, registration fees, and materials are covered by the club through sponsorships and university support.

💼 For Companies

  • Access to top student talent for recruiting
  • Brand visibility to tech-savvy audience
  • Host workshops and tech talks
  • Sponsor competition teams
  • Collaborate on research projects

🎓 For Academic Partners

  • Joint research opportunities
  • Guest lecture series
  • Lab equipment sharing
  • Student exchange programs
  • Conference collaborations

🤝 For Organizations

  • Community outreach programs
  • STEM education initiatives
  • Volunteer opportunities
  • Grant partnerships
  • Equipment donations
